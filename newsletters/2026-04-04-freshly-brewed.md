Anthropic — the AI company known for its Claude assistant — has quietly acquired a biotech startup called Coefficient Bio for $400 million. Coefficient was operating in 'stealth mode,' meaning it hadn't gone public with what it was building, which makes the price tag all the more eyebrow-raising.

This matters because it signals that the big AI players aren't just competing to build better chatbots — they're moving into healthcare and biology. When a top AI lab drops $400 million on a secretive biotech firm, it's a sign that AI-powered medicine and drug discovery are about to get a serious injection of resources and ambition.

**Big Tech Is Building Its Own Power Plants to Keep AI Running — and Not the Green Kind**

Meta, Microsoft, and Google are all investing in brand-new natural gas power plants to keep their AI data centers humming. Running AI takes an enormous amount of electricity — far more than most people realise — and the existing power grid simply can't keep up with demand.

The catch? Natural gas is a fossil fuel, and these are companies that have made very public pledges about going green. Building new gas infrastructure now could lock them into decades of carbon emissions, and it raises real questions about whether the AI boom is quietly undoing years of climate progress.

*The energy cost of AI is becoming impossible to ignore — and it may end up on your electricity bill, your news feed, and your conscience.*

# ☕ AI workflow of the week

**Stress-test your team's budget assumptions before the numbers go upstairs**

**Who this is for:** Finance managers, ops leads, department heads, business analysts

**Problem:** Every budget submission is built on assumptions that nobody challenges until something goes wrong. By the time a senior leader pokes a hole in your numbers, it's too late to look prepared.

**Tools:** ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini — free tier is fine

**How to run it (30–45 minutes)**

```
Step 1 — Gather your raw material

Pull up your budget plan, forecast, or business case — even a rough one. Jot down the 5–8 key assumptions baked into it (e.g. headcount stays flat, costs rise 3%, revenue grows 10%). You don't need the full spreadsheet, just the logic underneath it.

Example prompt setup: 'Here are the assumptions underpinning my Q3 budget: [list them]. My total ask is £X and the goal is Y.'

Step 2 — Prompt the AI to play CFO

Ask the AI to act as a sceptical CFO who has seen budgets fail and wants to pressure-test every assumption before approving. Tell it to identify which assumptions are weakest, what could go wrong, and what questions a senior leader is likely to ask.

Example prompt: 'You are a CFO reviewing this budget before sign-off. Identify the three riskiest assumptions, explain why each one could break, and list the two hardest questions a board member would ask about each.'

Step 3 — Refine by adding your context

Take the AI's challenge questions and feed back any context it's missing — your industry, last year's actuals, known constraints. Ask it to revise its critique and then suggest a one-line defence or mitigation for each weak point.

Example prompt: 'Now factor in that we missed our revenue target by 8% last year and headcount is already frozen. Update your risk list and give me a one-sentence rebuttal I can use for each challenge.'

Step 4 — Act: build your pre-mortem one-pager

Ask the AI to turn the final output into a short 'assumptions risk register' — a table with each assumption, its risk level, and your prepared response. Paste this into a doc and bring it to your next budget review as a pre-emptive slide or talking-note sheet.

Example prompt: 'Format this as a simple table: Assumption | Risk Level (High/Med/Low) | What could go wrong | Our mitigation. Keep each cell to one sentence.'
```

**The Prank Architect**

This week — let an AI become your delightfully devious prank strategist so you can cause chaos without losing any friends.

This prompt turns AI into a dramatic mastermind that helps you design a perfectly calibrated, harmless prank for someone you know — tailored to their personality and your relationship. Perfect for April Fool's Day, office culture moments, or anyone who just wants to cause a little harmless mayhem.

```
You are my theatrically dramatic prank architect — part evil genius, part best friend, with an ironclad rule that no one actually gets hurt, embarrassed beyond repair, or fired. Before you unleash your masterplan, ask me these questions one at a time: Who is the target and what is your relationship with them? What are their biggest quirks, pet peeves, or obsessions? What is your setting — office, home, group chat, or somewhere else? How bold do you want to go on a scale of 1 (gentle chaos) to 5 (legendary story they'll tell for years)? Once I've answered, deliver a fully staged prank plan with a catchy operation name, step-by-step instructions, a list of supplies or setup needed, and a predicted reaction — complete with your dramatic commentary on why this is a work of art.
```
