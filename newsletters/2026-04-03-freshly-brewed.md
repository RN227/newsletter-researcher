**OpenAI Just Bought a Popular Tech Podcast — and That Should Make You Curious**

OpenAI has acquired TBPN, a podcast that's become a go-to for Silicon Valley insiders and startup founders. The show will keep running independently, but it'll now sit under the watch of OpenAI's chief political strategist, Chris Lehane.

This isn't really about podcasting — it's about influence. AI companies are increasingly trying to shape the conversation around AI, not just build the technology. *AI companies are moving beyond products into media and politics — expect the line between AI news and AI marketing to get blurrier.*

**Anthropic Accidentally Nuked Thousands of Developers' Projects Chasing a Leak**

When some of Anthropic's internal source code leaked online, the company tried to scrub it by sending takedown notices — but the process went haywire, and thousands of unrelated developers' GitHub repositories got caught in the crossfire. Anthropic says it was an accident and has been walking the notices back.

This matters because it shows how messy things can get when AI companies — which hold enormous amounts of sensitive code and data — scramble to contain a leak. Innocent bystanders lost access to their own work, even briefly, because of someone else's crisis response.

*As AI companies grow more powerful, their mistakes — even accidental ones — can have real ripple effects on people and businesses that have nothing to do with the original problem.*

**Your AI Chatbot Has a Surprisingly Large Carbon Footprint — and It's Getting Bigger**

Meta is building a massive AI data center called Hyperion, and to keep it running, the company is firing up ten brand-new natural gas power plants. To put that in perspective, the energy it'll consume is roughly equivalent to powering the entire state of South Dakota.

Every time you use an AI tool — whether it's generating an image, summarising an email, or chatting with a bot — there's a real energy cost on the other end. The AI boom is quietly driving a surge in fossil fuel use, which is a tension worth watching as companies also talk up their green commitments.

*The environmental cost of AI is becoming impossible to ignore — and it may soon factor into how regulators, consumers, and investors judge these companies.*

# ☕ AI workflow of the week

**Use AI to cut impulse buys and compare options in 5 minutes**

**Who this is for:** Everyday shoppers, freelancers, parents, students — anyone who buys online

**Problem:** You either impulse-buy the first thing you see or fall into an endless comparison spiral and give up. Either way, you overspend or walk away empty-handed with nothing to show for the time lost.

**Tools:** ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini — free tier is fine. Any browser open to Amazon, AliExpress, Zalando, eBay, or wherever you shop.

**How to run it (30–45 minutes)**

```
Step 1 — Gather: Pull the product details that actually matter

Open the product page and copy three things: the price, the top 3–4 specs, and 2–3 reviews that reflect your use case (not the five-star cheerleaders — the honest middle ones). This raw material is what stops AI from giving you generic advice and forces it to react to your actual situation.

Example: Copy the product title, price, bullet-point specs, and paste in 2 reviews — one positive, one critical.

Step 2 — Prompt: Ask AI to stress-test the product and find alternatives

Paste everything into your AI chat and give it your real constraints — budget, delivery speed, what you actually need it for. You're not asking for a summary; you're asking it to play devil's advocate and scout the market for you.

Example prompt: "Here are the details for a product I'm considering: [paste specs, price, reviews]. I want to spend under €X, need it for [use case], and prefer fast delivery. List the pros and cons of this product, then suggest 2–3 better or cheaper alternatives at a similar price and explain why they might suit me better."

Step 3 — Refine: Compress it into a shortlist you can actually use

Once you have the pros, cons, and alternatives, ask AI to reformat the output into a clean 3-item shortlist with a single recommended winner and a one-line reason for each option. This gives you something screenshot-worthy to save, share, or sleep on — not a wall of text you'll ignore.

Example prompt: "Rewrite this as a 3-item shortlist. Label one as the clear winner and give a single sentence explaining why each option made the cut."

Step 4 — Act: Make a conscious decision, not an emotional one

Before you hit checkout, run one final prompt. Ask AI to give you one concrete reason to wait 24 hours and one concrete reason to buy right now — based only on the shortlist you built together. You're forcing the decision out of impulse mode and into deliberate choice, which is the whole point.

Example prompt: "Based on this shortlist, give me one specific reason I should wait 24 hours before buying and one specific reason it makes sense to buy today. Be direct."
```
